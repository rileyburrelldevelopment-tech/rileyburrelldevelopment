What EchoPath does

🗣️ Spoken guidance

Clear accessible navigation guidance for familiar and unfamiliar journeys.

📍 Saved places

Save important locations and return to them quickly when needed.

🧭 Navigation options

Customise distance units, guidance style, travel mode and navigation preferences.

🛠️ Tools and support

Helpful tools including voice commands, quick start guidance and support information.

♿ Accessibility-first

Designed with VoiceOver, large controls, high contrast and clear structure in mind.

🔒 On-device privacy

Location is used for app functionality and is not linked to your identity.

About the developer

EchoPath is developed by Riley Burrell, a blind developer from the UK. The app is being built from lived experience, with accessibility at the centre of every decision.

Riley Burrell Development focuses on creating apps, games and digital tools that are useful, accessible and designed with real users in mind.

Accessibility support

EchoPath is designed to support accessibility features including VoiceOver, larger text, Voice Control, sufficient contrast, dark interface and clear non-colour-only communication.

Accessibility is not an add-on for EchoPath. It is part of the foundation of the app.

Frequently asked questions

What is EchoPath?

EchoPath is an accessible navigation app designed for blind and visually impaired users.

Does EchoPath require an account?

No. EchoPath does not currently require an account or sign-in.

Why does EchoPath need location permission?

EchoPath uses precise location to provide navigation, distance information and guidance.

Is my location used for tracking?

No. EchoPath does not use precise location for tracking or advertising.

When will EchoPath be available?

EchoPath is currently being prepared for release and is coming soon to the App Store.
